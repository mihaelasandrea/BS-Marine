About:

Captain Bogdan Sandrea

Bogdan Sandrea started his sea life in 1992, as a deck cadet, and disembarked from his last vessel as a master in 2010. After he gained his chief officer ticket, in 1999, he continued working in that rank for approximately 10 years on vessels ranging from MR (the 35 kT’s from Maersk, a.k.a. the “R” class) up to VLCC size (the Saudi Arabian 300 kT’s). He had worked for various companies, thus gaining a priceless experience in dealing with different work environments.

Between 2000-2001, while he was employed by an Owner-Operator, he spent three months each year in preparing the Employer’s SIRE approach system, the result being at the customer’s satisfaction. He left the tanker world in 2005 for only a couple of months in order to sail as master on a freight ferry in the busy English Channel (Radicatel – near Port Jerome – to Southampton twice a day).

After gaining ship handling experience he returned to tankers on Aframax size ships – also short trips on one fitted with a bow thruster, carrying exclusively high hydrogen sulphide Mexican crude to USA. The two years described above provided him the experience of short trips (the shortest pilot to pilot sea time was 6 hours and the longest 42 hours) in two different parts of the world and also the understanding of the associated risks. He had never been involved in any kind of incident or accident but had been part of internal investigation committees a couple of occasions where he looked at reasons behind pollution incidents involving tankers.

He quit the sea life in 2010, due to family commitments, and became a SIRE inspector, being sponsored by an oil major. He voluntarily receives trainings on a regular basis, covering several areas of interest in the industry, staying up-to-date with the latest requirements and future regulations as well.

BS Marine

BS Marine was founded in 2010 in Rotterdam, the European shipping business centre, chosen due to its ideal location, between Amsterdam and Antwerp - the website was launched to celebrate 10 years of its existence. Over a hundred vessels are attended each year, most of them tankers, but also PCTC’s (pure car and truck carriers), general cargo, heavy lift, etc.

BS Marine covers successfully the ARA region (Amsterdam – Rotterdam – Antwerp) for various customers performing various works, ranging from SIRE and BIRE inspections, flag state inspections and audits and ending with STS assessments and investigations.

BS Marine is completely honest and impartial, being totally committed to the SIRE inspection programme, guided by its OCIMF Inspector Code of Ethics.

Services:

SIRE/BIRE/Report Assessments

In 1993, OCIMF established the SIRE Programme, which enabled its members to submit their ship inspection reports to OCIMF for distribution to the members and also certain qualifying non-OCIMF members. Assessing a ship report means carefully analyzing the SIRE/CDI report, thus practically walking into a virtual model of the vessel, producing a report which varies from a simplified to a more detailed one.

TMSA and Pre-TMSA

The first TMSA (Tanker Management and Self-Assessment) was published by the Oil Companies International Marine Forum (OCIMF) back in 2004.

It is a tool used by the tanker Operators for improving their Safety Management System

It consists in the verification of 12 elements, consisting in 4 stages each, various KPI’s being used.

Terminal Inspection and Compliance

The MTIS has been developed by OCIMF with the aim of filling the gaps that exist in the international standards for terminals, its goal being to ensure that all terminals worldwide reach a common high standard of safety and environmental protection. The vessels have to comply with various requirements e.g. trade, cargo, area specific to each of the terminals.

Find out more

STS Pre-Assessments

The STS operations are nowadays almost a routine operation, involving the transfer of oil chemicals or gas.

The STS plan available on board should cover all aspects of such operations, related but not limited to training, mooring equipment, fenders and hoses acceptance, personnel transfer, lighting during dark hours, weather, vapour balancing, etc.
